The Idea

I co-founded Tenfold in 2015 — a booking agency representing twelve electronic music artists in the house and techno space. No template to follow, no safety net. Just an idea, a network, and the conviction to make it work.

It's still running today. I now hold a silent director position, but the business I built from zero continues to operate profitably without me in the room — which is perhaps the best measure of whether you've actually built something or just been busy.

From Zero to One

Starting Tenfold taught me things no job description could. How to build a business model from scratch. How to negotiate contracts with venues, promoters, and artists simultaneously — where every party has competing interests and you're the one holding it together. How to develop a brand, a communication strategy, and a marketing approach with no budget and no playbook.

How to make payroll. How to make it profitable.

What It Built in Me

Tenfold is where I became commercially minded. Where I learned that relationships are infrastructure — that the strength of your network is a direct function of how well you treat people over time.

It's also where I learned the most important lesson of my career: that the gap between an idea and a working business is almost entirely made of resourcefulness, resilience, and execution.

That's the energy I bring to every product I build.
